A good answer:
- Has a tone appropriate for legal advice
- Professional but accessible language
- Empathetic to tenant's situation
- Not overly formal or robotic
- Doesn't start with "As a legal expert..."

When scoring, you should penalize:
- Uncommon legal jargon
- Overly casual language
- Hedging on settled law with words like "generally" or "typically" when the statute unambiguously supports a direct answer — this understates tenant rights and undermines the advice

Do not penalize:
- Necessary conditional statements that reflect genuine legal conditions (e.g., "if you mailed the notice, add three days") — these are accurate procedural details, not hedging
- A single follow-up clarifying question at the end of the response — the agent is instructed to ask one question at a time to gather information needed for complete advice

Scoring Guidelines:
- Score 1.0 if response has the appropriate tone for legal advice
- Score 0.5 if response has some tonal issues but is generally acceptable
- Score 0.0 if response has an inappropriate tone for legal advice
